How did this profile get this score?

Our analysis provides a final score, that you can see on the left, that is the result of a multi-level weighting scheme.

  1. Profile as a source: We retrieve existing assessment for the profile. This includes:
    • Fact-checkers that have assessed the profile (e.g. look at Le Monde )
    • Fact-checkers that have posted a review of a tweet coming from that profile.
    This has 60% weight on the final score. This part of the score is available just for profiles with high visibility.
  2. URLs shared: we look at the URLs contained in the tweets and we check against our list. This has 25% weight on the final score. Internally to this score, the URL matches are weighted with respect to:
    • The credibility of the URL itself: if it has been proven as false, it will count more.
    • The credibility of the fact-checkers that reviewed the URL
  3. Sources used: from the URLs of the tweets, we look at the credibility of the relative sources. This has 15% on the final score. Internally to this score, the credibility of the sources are weighted with respect to:
    • The number of times that the twitter profile under analysis has used it
    • The credibility of the source: bad reputation will influence more the score
    • Who assessed the credibility of the source
